Handkerchief Lasagna

Chef John's handkerchief lasagna is made with paper-thin homemade pasta sheets, folded over a delicate ricotta filling and a light amount of pasta sauce. These filled "handkerchiefs" are then layered to create one of the best versions of lasagna ever.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose or “00” flour
  • 3 large eggs
  • 12 oz ricotta cheese
  • 1 oz grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 large egg yolk
  • ¼ cup finely minced Italian parsley
  • 1 tsp kosher salt
  • ½ tsp freshly ground black pepper
  • Pinch of cayenne pepper
  • 3 cups prepared marinara sauce
  • 6 fresh basil leaves, torn
  • 4 oz shredded mozzarella cheese
  • ¼ cup grated Parmesan cheese (topping)

Steps

  1. Make pasta dough: heap flour, form a well, add eggs, and incorporate into a shaggy dough. Knead into a smooth ball (~3 min), rest 10–15 min, knead another ~4 min. Halve dough; refrigerate one half ≥2 hrs (freeze the rest).
  2. Prepare cheese filling: mix ricotta, Parmesan, egg yolk, parsley, salt, pepper, and cayenne; chill until needed.
  3. Preheat oven to 450 °F (230 °C).
  4. Roll dough into a large, thin rectangle, then cut lengthwise into 2 strips.
  5. Boil salted water; cook strips 1½ min, then shock in cold water. Cut each strip in half (4 pieces total).
  6. Reserve ½ cup cheese filling. Spread remaining cheese onto pasta pieces; top each with ~⅓ cup sauce and torn basil.
  7. Scatter shredded mozzarella over top (reserve ~¾ cup mozzarella).
  8. Fold each into a “handkerchief.” Sprinkle remaining Parmesan.
  9. Bake until golden and bubbly. Serve hot.
